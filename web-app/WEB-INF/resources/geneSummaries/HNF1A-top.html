HNF1A encodes a transcription factor that is highly expressed in the human liver, where it regulates several genes. A low-frequency variant in HNF1A -- found in some 2 percent of individuals of Mexican or Latino ancestry -- raises risk of type 2 diabetes by fivefold, the largest effect ever observed for a T2D variant found in more than 0.1 percent of the population. The same variant has been reported as a cause of the rare Mendelian form of diabetes known as MODY.
