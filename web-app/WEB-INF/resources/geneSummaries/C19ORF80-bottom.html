Betatrophin circulates in human plasma. It is as yet unclear whether it plays a role in human diabetes. A 2014 exome chip study examined fasting glucose levels in 13,754 Europeans and found no significant association between those participants’ fasting glucose levels and the low-frequency, protein-truncating C19orf80 variant rs145464906 (c.361C>T; p.Gln121*). In one cohort, plasma glucose did not differ between 23 carriers of rs145464906 (n = 23) and 10,620 non carriers (P=0.57); in a second cohort, 13 carriers had a mean fasting glucose of 88.2 mg/dl while noncarriers had mean glucose of 99.9 mg/dl (P=0.13).


The 2014 exome chip study did find a genome-wide significant association between rs145464906 and HDL cholesterol in people of European ancestry (P=5.5x10-13). European carriers of the variant had 10 mg/dl higher HDL-C (p=5 × 10−11), lower TG (−15%; p=0.003), and nonsignificantly lower LDL-C (−5.8 mg/dl; p = 0.13) than noncarriers of the variant. (There were too few African-American carriers of the variant for its potential effect to be accurately estimated in that ancestry group.) The authors noted that another, more common coding variant in C19orf80 (rs2278426; c.175C>T; p.Arg59Trp) has been associated with low LDL-C and low HDL-C. In addition, mouse and functional studies "have suggested that [C19orf80] may activateANGPTL3 and thereby regulate lipoprotein metabolism;” mice carrying a loss-of-function variant in the gene have very low triglyceride levels.
